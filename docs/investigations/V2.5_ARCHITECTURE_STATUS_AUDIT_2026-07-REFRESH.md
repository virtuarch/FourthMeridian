# Fourth Meridian — v2.5 Architecture Status Audit (2026-07 Refresh)

**Status:** Investigation only. No implementation. Brutally honest, grounded in the codebase as it exists today.
**Snapshot of the tree:** ~71k LOC (lib 25.4k / components 29.5k / app 15.8k), 74 API routes, 27 pages, 22 test files, **224 markdown docs** (63 investigations + 122 initiative docs). AI subsystem = **9,468 lines** (~37% of `lib`). Every monetary field in the schema is **`Float`** (zero `Decimal`).

## 2. Roadmap alignment

**On track architecturally; drifting toward over-engineering and architecture-ahead-of-product.**

The engineering *discipline* is ahead of most teams — but the *allocation* is drifting. Evidence: 224 governance docs and a 6+-slice, investigation-per-slice ritual to migrate ~10 audit producers (EV-1); a 9.5k-line AI subsystem that is mostly inert; an Atlas glass-material engine + Earth-background line of work with 10 dedicated investigation docs; and an SP-1 SpaceTemplate foundation that unlocks a **deferred** marketplace. Meanwhile the surfaces a user actually touches daily — the Brief, the Timeline, transactions — are still converging.

The drift from the original vision isn't in *quality*; it's in *sequencing*. You are building foundations beautifully and completing product journeys slowly. The vision ("a premium space to understand your financial life") is being served by plumbing faster than by experience.

## 3. Recent implementation review

- **SP-2 (authorization centralization)** — **Worth it. Scope correct. Not over-built.** Centralizing space authorization is core security and it shows in the clean `requireSpaceAction`/`requireSpaceRole` call sites. Keep.
- **KD-6 (encryption modernization / per-purpose HKDF)** — **Worth it, correct.** Blast-radius reduction on a shared key is real, unglamorous security value. Right-sized; don't touch again.
- **EV-1 (typed domain-event seam)** — **The seam was worth it; the process over-invested.** The design is right (no bus/queue, additive, `AuditLog` reused). But it took ~6 implementation slices + several investigations + a closure audit to migrate a handful of producers, and it delivered no user-visible value until T-1/T-2. Today I'd compress it to 2–3 slices with a fraction of the documents. The artifact is good; the ceremony was heavy.
- **Timeline Foundation T-1/T-2** — **Worth it, correct scope, the payoff moment.** This is where EV-1 finally becomes user value (invites, joins, check-ins appear). Right-sized. The only smell is the deliberate `invitedEmail`-holds-a-username hack now live in metadata.
- **Daily Brief work (improvements, account-count fix, tracked-accounts modal, simplification)** — **Worth it, but showing churn.** The sheer number of separate Brief tickets suggests over-tuning one surface. It's the right surface to perfect (daily-return hook), but it's time to **freeze** it and declare it done rather than keep iterating.
- **Spaces redesign / Liquid cards** — **Worth it for premium feel, watch the ROI.** Visual polish on the primary surface is defensible; the risk is that Atlas glass/Liquid-card R&D is absorbing effort that the *content* of those surfaces (real AI insight, transactions) needs more.
- **Daily Brief tracked-accounts modal tab** — **Small, fine.** No notes.

**Pattern across all seven:** the security/foundation items (SP-2, KD-6) were correctly scoped; the seam/UX items trended toward over-process (EV-1) or over-iteration (Brief). Nothing here was wasted, but the *marginal* slice was often lower-value than the first.

## 4. Codebase health — what's getting messy

- **Duplicate ` 2` directories (KD-13) — worse than a nuisance.** 14 of them, **untracked in git** (so harmless to prod), including **5 inside `lib/ai`** and 4 inside `docs/initiatives/d2`, plus `components/space/widgets 2`, `lib/providers/plaid 2`. These are sync-conflict artifacts — the repo lives in an iCloud/Finder-synced path (`~/Documents/Claude/Projects/...`) that keeps generating them. They pollute globs and navigation and signal a **development-environment** problem, not just a cleanup task.
- **AI subsystem sprawl.** 9.5k lines, with `annotations.ts` at 2,086. This is the one place in `lib` that has outgrown "readable at a glance" and needs decomposition before it grows further.
- **Documentation / investigation sprawl.** 224 markdown files (63 investigations, 122 initiative docs) for ~71k LOC. The decision records are genuinely excellent — but at this volume, coherence and discoverability decay, and the checklist-per-slice habit is itself a process tax. This audit is, ironically, doc #225.
- **Compatibility layers awaiting removal.** Legacy `Account`/`FinancialAccount` dual-FK (13 files), `plaidAccountId` fallback (9), D3 dual-write (8) — each gated on an "observation window" that has no owner or deadline and risks becoming permanent.
- **Naming residue.** The Workspace→Space rename left permanent `@map("workspaceId")` / `@map("WorkspaceDashboardSection")` DB names — a forever-mild dissonance between code and columns. Acceptable, but real.
- **What's clean:** top-level docs (ROADMAP correctly superseded by a single canonical STATUS.md), the retirement of `WorkspaceAccountShare` (enforced by a `no retired-model reads` guard test — genuinely well done), and the thin provider adapter.

## 5. Hidden technical debt (the subtle kind)

- **Money as `Float` (the headline latent bug).** Zero `Decimal` in the schema; every balance, amount, holding price/value, goal target, and snapshot sum is a float. Display rounding hides it today, but net-worth sums, debt-payoff progress, and snapshot deltas accumulate sub-cent drift that compounds with data volume. For a net-worth product this is the single most expensive thing to fix *later*.
- **Rate limiter off by default (KD-3).** `RATE_LIMIT_ENABLED` must be manually `true` in prod and **isn't in `.env.example`**. A security control that ships disabled and undocumented is effectively absent.
- **Permanent-shadow AI risk.** 23 shadow/observational hooks (KD-2 output validation, D6.3D selection planning, etc.) ship inert. Shadow mode is a fine *transition*, but with no promotion deadline it becomes a permanent parallel system that costs complexity and returns nothing.
- **Observation-window fallbacks that never close.** D2 `plaidAccountId` fallback and D3 dual-write are both "remove after prod observation" — with no trigger defined, they calcify.
- **Live misnamed metadata (T-1).** `invitedEmail` now stores a username by design decision; every consumer that later trusts that key inherits the lie.
- **Timeline dual-spelling aliases (5C).** Correctly deferred, but the alias set only grows until someone runs the observation-gated cleanup.
- **Direct `AuditLog` writers (36 files).** Expected post-EV-1 (non-domain producers stay direct), but it means the "typed seam" covers a minority of audit writes — worth being honest that EV-1 canonicalized the *Timeline-visible* subset, not the audit log as a whole.

## 6. Product audit

**What feels incomplete:** the *content* behind the polish. Transactions and Documents don't exist yet (the Timeline advertises their absence). The AI — 9.5k lines — computes largely in shadow and rarely surfaces a visible, trustworthy insight. The activity feed still mixes real rows with `isPreview` placeholder rows. Rate limiting is off.

**What would make it feel premium:** not more glass — more *truth surfaced*. One genuinely smart, correct, visible AI insight per day in the Brief would do more for premium feel than another card-material pass. Removing every "coming soon"/preview affordance (only show what's real) would do more than a new animation.

**Journeys that break the illusion:** the activity feed showing preview events next to real ones; an AI that clearly "knows" things (it's computing them) but won't say them; an invite that displays a username in a field labeled like an email. Each quietly tells the user "this is a prototype."

**Where architecture has out-run value:** the AI intelligence layer and the Atlas visual-engine R&D are the two biggest investments with the least proportional user-visible return today. EV-1's *process* (not its result) is the third.

## 7. Architecture priority re-rank (challenging the prior order)

Recommended new order, highest leverage first — and yes, this **down-ranks SP-1**, reversing the prior "SP-1 next" enthusiasm:

1. **MC1 — money/currency model.** Float → integer-cents or `Decimal`. Foundational correctness; the most painful thing to defer. **Do first.**
2. **JOB-1 — job substrate.** Unblocks async sync/webhooks/cron; the Plaid refresh + snapshot fan-out are begging for it; it's the real scalability ceiling. High leverage.
3. **D2 fallback removal + KD-13 dir cleanup (bundled hygiene).** Close the `plaidAccountId`/dual-write loops (or consciously accept them with a date), delete the 14 ` 2` dirs, and move the repo out of the synced folder. Cheap, high-hygiene, unblocks clarity.
4. **Search.** Now genuinely unblocked by the event seam — the first initiative that *cashes in* the EV-1 investment with user value.
5. **SP-1 SpaceTemplate.** Elegant and low-risk, but Phase-1 has **zero user value** and unlocks a deferred marketplace. Build it *small* only when a real template/marketplace feature is greenlit. Not a v2.5 priority.
6. **Planner promotion / PE-2/PE-3 perspectives / SN-1/SN-2 snapshots.** Enhancements on working systems; sequence after the above.
7. **Timeline 5C cleanup.** Correctly deferred; wait for the observation window. Lowest priority.

## 8. Blind spots (as lead architect)

- **What you're overlooking:** money is stored as Float. In a financial app, that's not a detail — it's the substrate. Everything above it is polish on possibly-imprecise numbers.
- **Where you're spending too much time:** investigation/checklist process and foundational seams. The governance is excellent but has crossed into ceremony — a 6-slice, doc-per-slice cadence for modest changes.
- **What you're avoiding:** the boring, high-stakes work — the money model, turning the rate limiter on, actually *removing* the compat fallbacks, and *promoting* the shadow AI into visible value. Seam-building is more satisfying than any of these, which is exactly why they're being deferred.
- **Decisions that get painful in v3:** (a) Float money, once there's reconciliation-grade data; (b) permanent shadow AI that never ships; (c) fallback branches that calcify because no window ever "closes"; (d) the repo living in a sync-conflict-prone folder; (e) DB columns permanently named `workspace`.

## 9. If you had one month — "Correctness, then Convergence"

One theme, three moves, deliberately narrow:

- **Week 1 — MC1 money model.** Migrate monetary storage to `Decimal`/integer-cents before more production data accrues. This is the highest-stakes, least-reversible fix; do it first, while it's still cheap.
- **Weeks 2–3 — Converge ONE journey to premium.** Pick the **Brief + Timeline "daily story"** (closest to done, and it's the daily-return hook). Promote *one* shadow AI feature into a visible, correct, trustworthy insight; remove every preview/`isPreview` affordance where a real producer now exists; make it feel finished end-to-end.
- **Week 4 — Hygiene & honesty.** Delete the 14 ` 2` dirs and move the repo off the synced path; close or consciously date-stamp the D2 fallbacks; turn the rate limiter on and document its flags.

**Why:** correctness before polish (fix the numbers), then make the one thing users touch daily actually feel complete (convert latent AI into visible value), then pay the cheap debt that's quietly compounding. **Explicitly not this month:** SP-1, more AI R&D, more seams, more Brief micro-tweaks.

## 10. What should NOT be touched

- **EV-1 event seam** — done enough. Stop adding slices; stop re-auditing it. It works; extend it only when a consumer needs a new event.
- **SP-2 authorization** — stable; leave it.
- **KD-6 encryption** — complete.
- **`WorkspaceAccountShare` retirement** — finished and test-enforced; never reopen.
- **`regenerateSpaceSnapshot` / snapshot pipeline** — works, isolated, idempotent.
- **Provider adapter thinness** — right-sized; do **not** expand it until a real second provider exists.
- **Daily Brief** — freeze it. It's had enough tickets; declare it done and stop revisiting.

---

## 11. Final verdict

- **Easier or harder to evolve?** **Easier at the seams** (events, authz, providers, templates), **slowly harder at the substrate** (Float money, calcifying compat layers, doc sprawl, sync-cruft). Net still positive — but the trajectory has a ceiling unless the debt loops start closing.
- **Is the architecture improving?** **Yes, genuinely.** The discipline is above average and the seams are real.
- **Are we building the right product?** **Mostly — but drifting.** The product needs *completion and correctness* more than it needs another foundation. You're one or two premium, finished journeys away from feeling like a product instead of a very well-architected platform.
- **Biggest missed opportunity:** the **AI subsystem** — 9.5k lines, mostly in shadow. It's the largest built asset returning the least visible value. Shipping one trustworthy insight from it is the highest-ROI move available.
- **Highest-leverage initiative after v2.5:** **MC1 (money correctness) paired with converging the Brief+Timeline journey to premium.** Not SP-1, not more AI R&D. Fix the numbers, then make the daily surface feel finished.

**Stop after the audit.**
